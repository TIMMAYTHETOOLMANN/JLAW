# Subpoena and Verification Target Index

## Purpose

This index identifies categories of records held by third-party vendors and internal departments that may be relevant to an investigative inquiry. Identification of a category here does not imply that any vendor or individual has acted improperly. These are records whose production would permit independent verification of claims under review.

All records requests should be coordinated with legal counsel. Descriptions use conservative language consistent with the investigative language guide.

---

## Target Categories

### 1. Beckwood Press — Fault and Safety Logs

**Entity type:** Equipment manufacturer / vendor

**Records categories:**
- Press fault logs and alarm histories for equipment supplied to the subject facility
- Error code records and operator-acknowledged fault events
- PLC (programmable logic controller) data exports covering the relevant time period
- HMI (human-machine interface) event logs
- Maintenance and service records for installed equipment
- Any safety incident reports or corrective action records submitted to or by the customer

**Relevance:** Records from press equipment may permit independent verification of claims regarding equipment safety status, fault frequency, and maintenance response.

---

### 2. Beckwood Press — HMI / PLC Telemetry

**Entity type:** Equipment manufacturer / vendor

**Records categories:**
- HMI screen-capture logs and operator interaction records
- PLC ladder logic versions and change histories
- Remote diagnostic session logs
- Engineering change orders relating to safety interlocks or guarding

**Relevance:** Telemetry and configuration records may indicate whether safety systems were active, modified, or bypassed during the relevant period.

---

### 3. Kiefel — Splice-Welder Activation Logs

**Entity type:** Equipment manufacturer / vendor

**Records categories:**
- Splice-welder cycle logs and activation histories
- Machine-state records for welding operations
- Fault and alarm histories

**Relevance:** Activation logs may permit verification of claims regarding equipment operation frequency, fault conditions, and any safety-related events during the relevant period.

---

### 4. Kiefel — HMI / PLC Diagnostic Records

**Entity type:** Equipment manufacturer / vendor

**Records categories:**
- HMI event and diagnostic logs
- PLC program versions and modification records
- Remote service and diagnostic session records
- Safety circuit and interlock configuration records

**Relevance:** Diagnostic records may indicate the operational and safety state of welding equipment during the relevant period.

---

### 5. Kwikool — Maintenance and Condensate Handling Documentation

**Entity type:** HVAC / cooling equipment vendor

**Records categories:**
- Maintenance visit logs and service records for cooling units installed at the subject facility
- Condensate management documentation (drain, collection, disposal records)
- Any records relating to water accumulation, leaks, or moisture events involving installed equipment
- Corrective action records

**Relevance:** Maintenance and condensate records may permit independent assessment of claims regarding environmental conditions, moisture management, and related safety or compliance matters.

---

### 6. Internal Maintenance Logs — Facility Operator

**Entity type:** Internal records (subject facility)

**Records categories:**
- Work order logs for all equipment maintenance during the relevant period
- Corrective and preventive maintenance records
- Lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedure records and compliance documentation
- Safety committee meeting minutes
- Internal incident and near-miss reports
- Equipment inspection records

**Relevance:** Internal maintenance records would permit verification of whether documented fault conditions or safety concerns were addressed, escalated, or resolved.

---

### 7. OSHA Safety Reports

**Entity type:** Regulatory agency (U.S. Department of Labor, Occupational Safety and Health Administration)

**Records categories:**
- Inspection records for the subject facility during the relevant period
- Citation records and penalty assessments
- Abatement verification records
- Any whistleblower complaint records (if publicly releasable)
- Fatality and severe injury reports

**Access pathway:** FOIA request to OSHA regional office; some records available through OSHA's public enforcement database.

**Relevance:** Regulatory inspection and citation records provide independent governmental assessment of safety conditions at the subject facility.

---

### 8. EPA Waste Classification Records

**Entity type:** Regulatory agency (U.S. Environmental Protection Agency)

**Records categories:**
- Hazardous waste determination records for materials generated at the subject facility
- Waste manifest records (Uniform Hazardous Waste Manifests)
- Any permit records or permit applications
- Violation and enforcement records
- Inspection reports

**Access pathway:** FOIA request to EPA regional office; some records available through EPA's ECHO enforcement database.

**Relevance:** Waste classification and manifest records would permit independent verification of claims regarding environmental compliance and waste handling practices.

---

### 9. SEC ESG Disclosure Support Files

**Entity type:** SEC filing issuer (internal records)

**Records categories:**
- Internal documentation underlying ESG disclosures made in 10-K filings, proxy statements, and standalone sustainability reports
- Data collection methodologies supporting reported metrics
- Third-party audit or assurance records for ESG claims
- Internal legal review records for ESG disclosure language

**Relevance:** Support file records would permit verification of whether published ESG disclosures are supported by underlying data and whether disclosed metrics accurately reflect operational practices.

---

### 10. Vendor Service Records — General

**Entity type:** All vendors providing equipment, maintenance, or services at the subject facility

**Records categories:**
- Service contracts
- Visit logs and technician notes
- Any records documenting safety-related concerns communicated to or from the facility operator

**Relevance:** Vendor service records may corroborate or contradict claims about equipment condition, maintenance frequency, and the communication of safety concerns.

---

## Notes

- This index reflects records categories only. It does not identify specific individuals as targets of investigation.
- Inclusion of a vendor or agency here reflects the potential relevance of their records to claims under review, not an allegation of wrongdoing.
- Records requests should be prioritized based on claim confidence scores in the claim verification matrix.
- Claims scored 1 in the matrix should not drive records requests until confidence is raised through other means.
