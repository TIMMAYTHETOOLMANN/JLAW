## Section 4: Established Findings

The following findings have been established through prior investigation work. Each must be **verified and expanded** during the year-by-year analysis. Do not contradict these findings without documentary evidence from SEC filings.

### Finding 1: Parker Cumulative Open-Market Sales (~$344M)
- **Fact:** Mark G. Parker executed approximately $344 million in open-market sales (Code S transactions only) across the investigation period
- **Source:** Aggregated Form 4 filings, CIK 0000320187
- **Note:** This figure excludes option exercises (Code M), gifts (Code G), and other non-sale disposition codes. The $565M figure that has appeared in some analyses includes all transaction types and must not be represented as open-market sales.

### Finding 2: Swoosh LLC Schedule 13D — Unamended Since June 2016
- **Fact:** Swoosh LLC (CIK 0001645433) has not amended its Schedule 13D since June 2016, a gap of approximately 9.7 years
- **Violation:** Rule 13d-2 requires prompt amendment upon any material change in facts (including ownership percentage changes, changes in plans/proposals, or changes in control arrangements)
- **Statutory Basis:** 15 U.S.C. § 78m(d); 17 CFR § 240.13d-2

### Finding 3: Exhibit 19 Self-Approval Loophole
- **Fact:** Nike's insider trading policy (Exhibit 19) designates a single "Clearance Director" for pre-clearance of trades — with no independent override mechanism
- **Significance:** CLO Leinwand sold $565K in NKE stock under a pre-clearance policy she herself administers, creating an inherent conflict of interest
- **Peer Comparison:** Most large-cap public companies require an independent compliance committee or outside counsel for officer pre-clearance

### Finding 4: Securities Fraud Class Action (292 Pages, 19 CWs)
- **Case:** In re Nike, Inc. Securities Litigation, D. Or. 3:24-cv-00974-AN
- **Status:** MTD pending; 292-page amended complaint; 19 confidential witnesses
- **Significance:** Provides external validation of MNPI patterns identified in JLAW analysis

### Finding 5: Travis Knight Late Form 4
- **Fact:** Travis Knight filed Form 4 on January 5, 2026, for a transaction dated December 22, 2025
- **Delay:** Approximately 6 business days late (Section 16(a) requires filing within 2 business days)
- **Statutory Basis:** 15 U.S.C. § 78p(a); Rule 16a-3(g)

### Finding 6: SEC Correspondence Gap (~12 Years)
- **Fact:** The last SEC CORRESP filing for Nike, Inc. was February 2014 (accession number 0000320187-14-000014)
- **Gap:** Approximately 12 years without SEC comment letter correspondence
- **Significance:** For a company of Nike's market capitalization and filing complexity, this gap is statistically anomalous and may indicate insufficient SEC review attention
- **CORRECTION:** The gap is ~12 years from February 2014, NOT 14 years from December 2011

### Finding 7: Section 16(a) Compliance Disclosure Toggle Pattern
- **Fact:** Nike's proxy statement (DEF 14A) Section 16(a) compliance disclosure follows an ABSENT/ABSENT/PRESENT/ABSENT/PRESENT/PRESENT/PRESENT pattern across 7 proxy filing years
- **Significance:** The alternating pattern suggests inconsistent internal compliance monitoring; years with ABSENT disclosure should be examined for unreported late filings

### Finding 8: September 18-19, 2024 MNPI Sequence
- **Fact:** On September 18, 2024, Nike filed an 8-K announcing bylaws amendments. On September 19, 2024, Nike announced the CEO transition (Donahoe out, Hill in)
- **Significance:** The bylaws amendment preceding the CEO announcement by one day creates a potential MNPI sequence — individuals aware of both events had material nonpublic information about the leadership transition
- **Investigation Priority:** Examine all Form 4 transactions in the 30 days preceding September 19, 2024

### Finding 9: Named Defendant Friend Adopted 10b5-1 Plan While MTD Pending
- **Fact:** CFO Matthew Friend adopted a Rule 10b5-1 trading plan while the motion to dismiss in the securities fraud class action was still pending
- **Significance:** Adopting a trading plan during active litigation, while a named defendant, raises questions about the plan's good faith defense under the amended Rule 10b5-1 requirements
- **Statutory Basis:** 17 CFR § 240.10b5-1(c)(1)(ii) (2023 amendments)

### Finding 10: CLO Leinwand Self-Administered Trading
- **Fact:** CLO Ann Miller Leinwand sold $565K in NKE stock under an insider trading policy that she herself administers as Clearance Director
- **Significance:** Inherent conflict of interest — the individual responsible for pre-clearing insider trades is trading under the very policy she oversees
- **Governance Gap:** No independent review mechanism for officer-level transactions

## Section 7: Evidence Standards

### 7.1 The "Ten Toes Down" Rule

Every single claim in every output document must satisfy ALL of the following:

1. **ACCESSION NUMBER:** Exact 18-digit accession number (e.g., 0000320187-24-000044)
2. **DATE:** Exact filing date in YYYY-MM-DD format
3. **LOCATION:** Page, section, exhibit, or XML element path within the filing
4. **STATUTE:** Exact USC/CFR citation (e.g., 15 U.S.C. § 78p(a), Rule 16a-3)
5. **PARTIES:** Full legal names of all involved individuals/entities
6. **CORROBORATION:** At least one independent second source confirming the claim

### 7.2 Claim Classification

| Classification | Meaning | Action |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| **VERIFIED** | Meets all 6 evidence standard criteria | Include in all submission documents |
| **SINGLE-SOURCE** | Meets 5 of 6 criteria (missing corroboration) | Flag as `[SINGLE-SOURCE — REQUIRES VERIFICATION]`; exclude from submission bundles until verified |
| **UNVERIFIED** | Meets fewer than 5 criteria | Do not include in any output document; note in investigation log only |

### 7.3 FRE 902 Compliance

All evidence referenced in submission documents must meet Federal Rules of Evidence Rule 902 standards for self-authentication:
- SEC EDGAR filings are self-authenticating as domestic public documents (FRE 902(5))
- XBRL data authenticated via SEC EDGAR validation
- Form 4 XML authenticated via EDGAR submission receipt
- Evidence chain integrity maintained via SHA-256, SHA3-512, and BLAKE2b hashing with RFC 3161 timestamping
